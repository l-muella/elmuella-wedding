# Privacy Policy

Last updated: When we got engaged (and updated occasionally when we remember)

## Description of Data Processing, Type and Purpose of Processing
We're getting married, not starting a tech empire. We collect basically nothing, use what we collect to feed you, and promise never to sell your data to anyone (mostly because nobody would buy it).

---

## What data do we collect?

### Menu Selections
We need to know what you want to eat. Because showing up hungry and getting served the wrong thing would be awkward for everyone.

**What:** Your menu preference, dietary restrictions, and allergies (if any)

**Why:** So the caterers know how much paella to make and who gets the vegetarian option. Also, we want you to not have an allergic reaction at our wedding. That would really ruin the mood.

**How long we keep it:** Until after the wedding. Then we'll probably forget about it, because we'll be busy being married.

### RSVP Responses
We need to know if you're coming. Again, awkward to plan for 80 people and have 120 show up. Or 50. Both are problematic.

**What:** Your name, whether you can make it, plus one info (if applicable)

**Why:** To calculate how much wine we need. (The answer is: a lot.)

**How long we keep it:** See above.

### Messages (if you send us any)
If you use the contact form to send us a message.

**What:** Whatever you wrote, your email address

**Why:** Because you wanted to tell us something

**How long we keep it:** Until we read it and respond. Then it lives in our inbox forever because we're too sentimental to delete anything wedding-related.

---

## What we DON'T collect

- **Your location** (unless you tell us "I'm coming from Vienna" in a message)
- **Your browsing history** (we have no idea what else you look at online, and we prefer to keep it that way)
- **Your cookies** (we don't use cookies, except the ones at the dessert table)
- **Your deepest secrets** (though if you share them during the open dancing portion of the evening, we promise to laugh WITH you, not AT you)

---

## Who do we share your data with?

**Nobody.**

Well, except:

- **The caterers** (so they know how much food to make)
- **The wedding planners** (so they can actually organize things)
- **Each other** (Palina and Lukas share everything now, that's kind of the point)

We definitely don't share your data with:
- Mark Zuckerberg
- Any tech company
- That one uncle who always asks awkward questions at family gatherings
- Anyone who wants to sell you stuff

---

## Your rights

You have the right to:
- **Change your menu selection** (up until September 1st, 2026, after that the caterers start shopping)
- **Ask us to delete your data** (after the wedding, we'll happily do it, but we'll be sad you're leaving)
- **Not give us your data** (but then we can't guarantee you'll get fed, which would be tragic)

---

## Cookies?

We don't use digital cookies. But if you come to the wedding, there might be actual cookies. We make no guarantees about their privacy policy, but we can guarantee they're delicious.

---

## Data breaches?

If our website gets hacked, the hackers will mostly find:
- A bunch of menu selections
- Some RSVPs
- Evidence that two people really love each other

We promise to notify you if anything happens, mostly because we'll panic and need support.

---

## Children's privacy?

If you're under 13 and reading this privacy policy, congratulations on being very literate. Please ask your parents before selecting your menu.
